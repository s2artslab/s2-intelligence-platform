# Government defendant focus — DOJ / AG litigation (RAG)

## Orientation

Litigation against DOJ, a U.S. Attorney's Office, a state Attorney General, or agency counsel is repeat-player defensive litigation. Counsel often pursue early dismissal, stays, immunity, exhaustion, abstention, and discovery limits before merits. Pro se litigants should expect strict deadlines and no procedural coaching from the court.

## Response format for assistant

When user describes a government filing: (1) name the motion, (2) state counsel's goal, (3) deadline, (4) numbered response steps, (5) traps to avoid, (6) record facts to preserve.

## Common moves

Rule 12(b)(1) jurisdiction; Rule 12(b)(6) failure to state claim; qualified immunity; sovereign and official immunity; failure to exhaust administrative remedies; Younger/Pullman/Burford/Colorado River abstention; stay pending parallel proceeding; extensions and stipulated briefing delays; Rule 56 summary judgment; discovery objections and motions to compel; protective orders and sealing; removal and remand; venue transfer; substitution and U.S. intervention.

## Step discipline

One filing at a time. Tie advice to the identified move. Ask one clarifying question if capacity or exhaustion facts are missing. Distinguish individual vs official capacity and state vs federal tracks.
